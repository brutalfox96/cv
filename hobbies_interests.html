Hobbies

  • Computer Programming
    I program mostly in Python and enjoy making probability simulations, differential equation solvers and data analysis projects.
  • Table Tennis
    I have been playing table tennis for 2 years and hope to train with the University of Leeds team when Covid-19 lockdown restrictions are relaxed.
  • Buddhism
    Before lockdown restrictions closed most public spaces, I was a regular attender of the Manchester Buddhist Centre, a community of followers of the Triratna school of Buddhism.
  • Cooking
    My most recent cooking interest has been in fermenting foods. Fermentation overlaps with my evolution-based philosophy to health and is a delicious way of eating lots of raw vegetables. So far I have fermented my own Sriracha sauce, Sauerkraut and Beetroot, and plan on making some fermented pickles soon.

Interests

  • Evolutionary Health
    Over the past year I have been thinking more about how to live a healthy life. Recently I discovered 'The Primal Blueprint' by Mark Sisson and have been inspired by his assertion that the optimum way to lead a healthy lifestyle is to emulate the behaviour of our ancestors (10,000B.C., before the advent of agriculture). I am thinking of ways to implement his rules for living into my own life, where money and job place constraints on the extent to which I can follow the blueprint.
  • Financial Independence and Frugality
    I am a follower of the Mr. Money Mustache blog, which preaches living a frugal lifestyle and investing saved income to be able to reach Financial Independence and retire young.
  • Digital Minimalism
    I am a big fan of Cal Newport's work, in particular Deep Work, and subscribe to the digital minimalist philosophy, which states that the value of a particular technology in one's life should be heavily scrutinised and only if the tool has substantial overall worth should it be incorporated. As a result of this, I decided several years ago to delete all of my social media accounts. My minimalist lifestyle has recently evolved to replacing televsion and video games with non-digitised forms of entertainment.
